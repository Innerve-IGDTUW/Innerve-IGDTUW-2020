MECHMASTER

From Robotics to Thermodynamics, from NASA to Tesla, Mechanical Engineering is a fascinating stream that unfurls a plethora of opportunities.

Test your Mechanical Engineering prowess with Mechmaster. It is a quiz-based event consisting of 4 rounds.

The first round will be a quiz on basic mechanical questions followed by two rounds testing your knowledge on various companies. In the fourth and final round, a rapid-fire round, we will ask short technical questions. The team with the maximum score at the end wins the round. You can participate individually or in a team of two. The event will be held on Quizziz and online meeting platforms.

Now is the time to fuel your memory and let the momentum build!

Rules:

1. Any kind of cheating or Plagiarism won’t be entertained.
2. The decision of innerve team shall be treated as final.
3. Participants must ensure that they have a good internet connection.

2. Screen sharing and video must be on.

7. Decision of the OC will be final.

MECHATHON

An engineer gives solutions to problems varying widely in their applications and significance.

If you wish to enhance your problem-solving skills then come and participate in Mechathon. The participants will be provided with critical real-life problems. Working in teams of 2 to 6 students, they will come up with either hardware or software solutions. The problem statements will be provided 25 days before the event. The teams can choose to work on any one of them and develop an ingenious solution for it. Prototypes and design solutions are allowed. The teams will have to present their idea on the day of the event where it will be judged based on its relevance, feasibility, and innovation.

So, gear up engineers-to-be and activate your skills.
Showcase your abilities to your full potential.

Rules:

1. There is no restriction in the number of teams participating from each Institute.
2. Each team should comprise a maximum of 6 members including the team leader.
3. Valid id proofs(College ID and Aadhar Card) must be submitted by each member of the group.
4. Webcam and microphones must be on at all times while presenting the solution.
5. The teams cannot make any changes in the application once they have submitted the registration amount.
6. The teams must adhere to the deadlines strictly. No applications will be entertained after the deadline.
7. The solution cannot contain content that is sexually explicit, violent, derogatory of any ethnic, racial, gender, religious, professional or age group, which violates any law, or which communicates a message inconsistent with the positive good will to which we wish to associate.

DECRYPT IT

The world is full of wonders.
We are all on a hunt for adventure.
Decrypt It is an Escape Room that will lead you through a trail of mazes, puzzles, and codes.

You have to find the clue and unlock the door that will direct you to the next clue to escape. Finish each round to unlock the next round. The slower you are in breaking out, the more difficult each puzzle will become. The participant that escapes the room in the least amount of time will be the winner. We guarantee prize money for the top escapers!

So, gear up, loosen your mental muscles, exercise your brains, and get on with this nerve-wracking adventure.

RULES AND GUIDELINES:

1. All the communications will be departed through a telegram group.
2. The event is open to all students having a verified identity card.
3. There will be a specific time limit for the participants to finish the game.
4. The team who is able to escape the room in the least time will be the winner.
5. Breach of deadline and/or plagiarism will result in disqualification.
6. It is necessary to attempt all the questions. However, there is no negative marking for the wrong answers.
7. In case of a tie, we will take the completion timing into account.
8. The form will be mailed only to the player 1 of each team. He /She can share it with their partner (if registered in a team of two). However, only player1 can submit the responses.
9. In case of any query or discrepancy, the organisers decision will be considered in the final scenario.
10. Teams must be present half an hour prior to the start of their respective event.
11. The participants should have good internet connectivity. However, if a team loses their internet connectivity in the middle of playing the game, they won't be allowed to compete with other teams further.
12. The participants are recommended to finish their game 5 minutes prior to the whole time given to them so that they don't face any problem regarding submission at the end moment.
